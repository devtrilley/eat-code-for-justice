What is Food Insecurity

Food insecurity is when a person or family doesn't have consistent access to enough food for an active, healthy life. It can happen for many reasons—like job loss, low wages, unexpected bills, or health problems—that make it hard to afford the food they need. It's more common than people think, and many families face tough decisions about whether to pay for rent, medicine, or food. Food insecurity isn’t just about feeling hungry; it’s also about not knowing where your next meal will come from, or worrying that your food won't last until you have money again.

If you or someone you know is experiencing food insecurity, it’s important to remember that it’s not your fault and you’re not alone. Life can be unpredictable, and many people go through this at some point. Feeling ashamed or guilty only adds to the burden, and it’s okay to ask for help. There are many resources, like food banks, community programs, and local organizations, that exist to support people in these situations. You deserve to have enough to eat, and reaching out for assistance is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Facts About Child Hunger in America

According to the latest estimates, as many as 14 million children in the United States live in "food insecure" homes. That phrase may sound mild, but it means that those households don't have enough food for every family member to lead a healthy life.
